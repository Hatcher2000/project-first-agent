# NEXT WEEK IN TIME

### 22 JUN
**On this day — Monday, June 22, 2026**
*This Monday marks a historical cocktail of American foundational pillars, from legal structures and vital social programs to the birth of a beloved frozen treat. We'll also remember pivotal environmental awakenings and the sheer power of music.*

**Celebrations & Holidays**
- **National Onion Ring Day:** A crispy, golden tribute to the unsung hero of the diner menu. Did you know the first published recipe for "French Fried Onions" appeared in a New York cookbook in 1933?
- **National Kissing Day:** Pucker up! This day encourages expressing affection through the simple, yet profound, act of a kiss. A fun fact: the scientific term for kissing is philematology.
- **National Chocolate Eclair Day:** Indulge in the elegant oblong pastry filled with cream and topped with icing. Its name, "eclair," is French for "flash of lightning," perhaps due to how quickly it's devoured!
- **National HVAC Tech Day:** A toast to the unsung heroes who keep our homes and workplaces perfectly temperate. Without them, our summers would be sweltering and our winters, well, decidedly un-cozy.
- **National Limoncello Day:** Sip on this bright, zesty Italian lemon liqueur, typically served chilled as an after-dinner digestivo. It's a taste of the Amalfi Coast right in your glass.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
- In 1940, the **very first Dairy Queen store** opened its doors in Joliet, Illinois, right along historic Route 66. It forever changed the landscape of American roadside treats, proving soft-serve could be a hard hit.
- The grim reality of labor disputes hit southern Illinois hard in 1922, as the second day of the **Herrin Massacre** saw striking miners attack non-union replacement workers. A stark reminder of the often-bloody fight for workers' rights.

**US & World Historical Events**
- Back in 1870, the U.S. Congress saw fit to establish the **Department of Justice**, setting the stage for the country's chief law enforcement agency. Imagine trying to coordinate federal legal matters without it!
- A turning point for American veterans arrived in 1944 when President Roosevelt signed the **G.I. Bill** into law. This monumental act provided crucial benefits for returning WWII servicemen, reshaping American education and the middle class.
- The long, brutal **Battle of Okinawa** finally concluded in 1945, marking one of World War II's bloodiest chapters. Its end signaled the intense, costly path to victory in the Pacific.
- Patriotism took a more formal stance in 1942 as the U.S. Congress officially adopted the **Pledge of Allegiance**. It became a daily ritual in classrooms and public gatherings, embedding itself into the American experience.
- The environmental movement gained crucial momentum in 1969 when Cleveland's **Cuyahoga River caught fire**, a shocking spectacle that brought national attention to water pollution. A stark visual wake-up call that sparked real change.
- Our celestial curiosity was piqued in 1973 when the **first Skylab crew** splashed down safely in the Pacific after a successful 28-day mission. This early foray into orbital living laid crucial groundwork for future space exploration.
- The world watched in 1938 as American boxing legend **Joe Louis knocked out Germany's Max Schmeling** in a highly charged rematch, retaining his heavyweight title and delivering a powerful symbolic victory.
- The celebrated **Joni Mitchell** gifted the world her iconic fourth album, "Blue," in 1971. A deeply personal and influential work, it solidified her status as one of music's most profound songwriters.

### 23 JUN
**On this day — Tuesday, June 23, 2026**
*Today, we celebrate ingenuity, civil rights, and the enduring appeal of pop culture. From the clicks of a revolutionary machine to groundbreaking social legislation and cosmic journeys, this Tuesday is packed with milestones.*

**Celebrations & Holidays**
- **National Detroit-Style Pizza Day:** Dig into a square, deep-dish pizza with a thick, chewy crust and caramelized cheese edges. This Motor City marvel truly stands out in the pizza pantheon.
- **National Hydration Day:** Raise a glass (of water!) to staying well-hydrated. This simple act is vital for health, energy, and overall well-being.
- **National Pink Day:** A vibrant celebration of all things pink, from flamingos to bubblegum. It's a day to embrace this cheerful and often playful hue.
- **International Olympic Day:** Commemorating the birth of the modern Olympic Games in 1894, this day encourages participation in sport and promotes the Olympic ideals of excellence, friendship, and respect.
- **International Women in Engineering Day:** A vital observance recognizing the achievements of women in engineering and encouraging more girls to consider careers in this crucial field.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
- In 1888, the Republican convention in Chicago made history when abolitionist **Frederick Douglass received a vote** for U.S. president. This momentous nod made him the first Black candidate to have his name placed in nomination.

**US & World Historical Events**
- In 1868, Christopher Latham Sholes received a patent for his "Type-Writer," an invention that would fundamentally **revolutionize communication and office work**. Imagine a world without the clickety-clack!
- A pioneering feat of aviation unfolded in 1931 as **Wiley Post and Harold Gatty** departed New York, embarking on the first successful round-the-world flight in a single-engine plane. True global adventurers.
- A landmark moment for women's rights came in 1960 when the U.S. Food and Drug Administration approved **Enovid, the world's first combined oral contraceptive pill**. Its impact on society and individual autonomy was, and remains, immense.
- The fight for gender equality in education took a giant leap forward in 1972 with the signing of **Title IX**. This legislation prohibits sex discrimination in any federally funded education program, opening countless doors.
- The Caped Crusader flew onto the big screen in 1989 with the release of **Tim Burton's "Batman,"** starring Michael Keaton. This dark, stylish take on the comic book hero ushered in a new era of superhero blockbusters.
- In 2015, NASA's **Mars Odyssey spacecraft** completed its astounding 60,000th orbit of the Red Planet. This long-serving probe has provided invaluable data, continually expanding our understanding of Mars.
- Ice rinks around the world became a smoother place in 1953 when **Frank J. Zamboni** was granted a patent for his innovative ice resurfacer. Hockey players and figure skaters everywhere owe him a debt of gratitude.
- Proving once again that sometimes a walk is as good as a hit, **Barry Bonds stole his 500th career base** in 2003. This made him the first player in MLB history to boast both 500 home runs and 500 steals, a testament to his unique all-around talent.

### 24 JUN
**On this day — Wednesday, June 24, 2026**
*Today brings us a whirlwind tour through history, from early North American discoveries and pivotal moments in the Cold War to space-faring heroines and animated royalty. And yes, flying saucers make an appearance!*

**Celebrations & Holidays**
- **National Pralines Day:** Indulge in these sweet confections made of nuts (usually pecans or almonds) and caramelized sugar. Whether crisp European or creamy Southern, they're a delightful treat.
- **World UFO Day:** Keep your eyes on the skies! This day celebrates all things extraterrestrial and encourages discussion and awareness of unidentified flying objects.
- **Swim a Lap Day:** A perfect excuse to dive into the nearest pool, lake, or ocean. Whether for exercise or just cooling off, it's a refreshing way to mark the day.
- **Midsummer:** Celebrated in various cultures around the world, this day often marks the summer solstice, the longest day of the year, with traditions ranging from bonfires to flower crowns.
- **International Fairy Day:** A whimsical celebration of the magical, ethereal beings from folklore. It's a day to embrace enchantment, wonder, and perhaps, a little bit of pixie dust.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
- In 1862, former U.S. President **Martin Van Buren** passed away in Kinderhook, New York. His nickname, "Old Kinderhook," is often credited with popularizing the term "OK."
- The 24th Illinois Infantry Regiment demonstrated its mettle during the Civil War, advancing toward the enemy posted at Tullahoma. A reminder of Illinois's significant contribution to the Union cause.

**US & World Historical Events**
- A foundational moment in North American history: in 1497, Italian explorer **John Cabot, sailing for England, landed on what is now Newfoundland**. He laid early claims for future colonization.
- A crucial political transition in 1940 saw **TV cameras used for the first time** at a political convention as the Republicans gathered in Philadelphia. A precursor to the televised political landscape we know today.
- The Cold War began to truly chill in 1948 as the **Soviet Union initiated the Berlin Blockade**, cutting off all overland access to West Berlin. This brazen move set the stage for one of history's most iconic airlifts.
- In 1949, the small screen ushered in a new genre with **"Hopalong Cassidy," the first television western**, starring William Boyd. It quickly became a cultural phenomenon, especially among younger audiences.
- A landmark moment in space history occurred in 1983 as the **Space Shuttle Challenger, carrying America's first woman in space, Sally Ride**, completed its historic mission. A monumental step for both space exploration and gender equality.
- Disney captivated audiences worldwide in 1994 with the release of its animated epic, **"The Lion King."** This vibrant tale of courage, destiny, and catchy tunes became an instant classic, dominating the box office and Broadway.
- Tennis fans witnessed history in 2010 when the **longest professional tennis match ever** concluded at Wimbledon. American John Isner finally defeated Nicolas Mahut after an astonishing 11 hours and 5 minutes spread over three days.
- In 1947, Kenneth Arnold made the **first widely reported UFO sighting** near Mount Rainier, Washington, giving rise to the enduring term "flying saucer." The skies (and our imaginations) haven't been quite the same since.

### 25 JUN
**On this day — Thursday, June 25, 2026**
*Today, we reflect on pivotal moments in American governance and the Wild West, alongside cultural touchstones in music and film. From constitutional ratifications to iconic performances and the painful losses of legends, this Thursday is a profound journey through time.*

**Celebrations & Holidays**
- **National Catfish Day:** Dive into some fried catfish, a Southern culinary staple. This day celebrates the delicious freshwater fish and its role in American cuisine.
- **Bourdain Day:** A tribute to the late, great chef, writer, and traveler Anthony Bourdain. Fans celebrate by sharing a meal, a drink, and memories of his adventurous spirit.
- **National Bomb Pop Day:** Cool off with this iconic red, white, and blue rocket-shaped ice pop. A true taste of American summer since the 1950s.
- **National Work From Home Day:** A timely celebration for the modern workforce, recognizing the flexibility and challenges of remote work. Perhaps you're celebrating it right now!
- **Global Beatles Day:** A worldwide celebration of the timeless music and cultural impact of The Fab Four. The date marks their first live global satellite television broadcast in 1967.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
- In Milwaukee, Wisconsin, an exhibition titled **"Strike at Allis Chalmers and Other Wisconsin Stories"** opens today, exploring the rich and often challenging history of labor struggles in the state.
- The Wisconsin Historical Society is hosting **"A Slice of History,"** an event specifically crafted for Gen Z to delve into civics, democracy, and history right in their headquarters.

**US & World Historical Events**
- In 1788, **Virginia ratified the U.S. Constitution**, officially becoming the 10th state and a critical voice in the fledgling United States. A momentous step towards cementing the new nation.
- The American West was forever changed in 1867 with the patenting of the **first barbed wire** by Lucien B. Smith. This simple invention revolutionized ranching and land demarcation.
- A dramatic and tragic chapter in American history unfolded in 1876 at the **Battle of the Little Bighorn**, where Lt. Col. George Armstrong Custer and his men met their end. "Custer's Last Stand" became an enduring, if controversial, legend.
- The post-WWII world was irrevocably altered in 1950 as the **Korean War began** with the invasion of South Korea by North Korea. A conflict that set the stage for decades of geopolitical tension.
- The U.S. Supreme Court delivered a pivotal ruling in 1962, declaring the use of **unofficial, non-denominational prayer in public schools unconstitutional**. A landmark decision affirming the separation of church and state.
- Music history was made in 1967 when **The Beatles performed "All You Need Is Love"** live on "Our World," the first-ever worldwide satellite television broadcast. A message of peace beamed across the globe.
- The world lost an icon in 2009 with the passing of **Michael Jackson**. The King of Pop's death sparked a global outpouring of grief, reflecting his unparalleled influence on music and culture.
- Prince unleashed his legendary sixth studio album, **"Purple Rain,"** in 1984. More than just an album, it was a cultural phenomenon, spawning a hit film and cementing his status as a musical genius.

### 26 JUN
**On this day — Friday, June 26, 2026**
*As we wrap up the week, we look back at milestones that shaped global cooperation, consumer culture, and American legal rights, alongside the final acts of music and sports legends. And for a bit of future fun, prepare for some "Jackass."*

**Celebrations & Holidays**
- **National Chocolate Pudding Day:** A creamy, comforting classic gets its due. Whether you prefer it warm or cold, from a box or homemade, chocolate pudding is a universally loved treat.
- **National Food Truck Day:** Roll up to your favorite mobile eatery and celebrate the diverse, delicious, and convenient world of food trucks. They've revolutionized casual dining across the nation.
- **National Barcode Day:** Scan this! Today honors the ubiquitous Universal Product Code (UPC) and its role in modern retail. It's truly amazing how a few lines can unlock so much information.
- **National Beautician's Day:** A day to appreciate the talented professionals who help us look and feel our best, from hair stylists to nail technicians. They truly work wonders!
- **National Take Your Dog to Work Day:** A delightful day where offices around the country welcome canine companions. It's a chance to boost morale and maybe even fetch some inspiration.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
- In a controversial move in 1893, Illinois Governor John P. Altgeld issued **pardons for three anarchists** convicted after the infamous Haymarket Riot. A decision that stirred strong public and political reactions.
- The Wisconsin Historical Society offers its **"Step Into History: Behind the Scenes Tours"** today. It's a fantastic opportunity to peek behind the curtain and learn about the preservation of Wisconsin's architectural treasures.

**US & World Historical Events**
- A cornerstone of international relations was laid in 1945 as the **United Nations Charter was signed by 50 nations** in San Francisco. This historic pact established the UN, aiming to foster peace and cooperation worldwide.
- The tense drama of the Cold War escalated in 1948 with the start of the **Berlin Airlift**, initiated by Western allies to supply West Berlin after the Soviet blockade. An incredible logistical feat of airborne defiance.
- North America's Great Lakes gained a vital connection to the world's oceans in 1959 with the official opening of the **St. Lawrence Seaway**. This engineering marvel transformed trade and travel.
- Standing before the Berlin Wall in 1963, U.S. President John F. Kennedy delivered his iconic **"Ich bin ein Berliner" speech**. A powerful declaration of solidarity and a defiant stand against tyranny.
- Consumer culture took a revolutionary leap in 1974 when the **Universal Product Code (UPC) was scanned for the very first time** at a Marsh Supermarket in Troy, Ohio. A pack of Wrigley's Juicy Fruit gum ushered in the era of barcode efficiency.
- The King made his final bow in 1977 as **Elvis Presley gave his last concert** in Indianapolis, Indiana. A poignant end to the performing career of one of music's most electrifying and enduring legends.
- A landmark victory for civil rights occurred in 2015 when the U.S. Supreme Court, in its **Obergefell v. Hodges decision, legalized same-sex marriage** across the entire country. A truly historic moment for equality.
- Looking ahead to next year, **"Jackass: Best and Last,"** the fifth and final main installment in the beloved, chaotic film series, is slated for theatrical release in the United States. Prepare for one last dose of irreverent stunts and outrageous humor.
