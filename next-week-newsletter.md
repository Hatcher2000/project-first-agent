# NEXT WEEK IN TIME

### 22 JUN
**On this day — Monday, June 22**
From iconic Chicago firsts to the quiet beginnings of modern environmentalism, this Monday packs a surprisingly punchy historical wallop.

**Celebrations & Holidays**
-   **National Onion Ring Day (US):** Celebrate this crispy, golden, battered delight. Just try to keep the tears to a minimum.
-   **National Chocolate Eclair Day (US):** Proof that pastry cream and chocolate glaze make for a perfectly balanced meal.
-   **World Rainforest Day:** A global shout-out to the planet’s lungs—let's keep them breathing.
-   **HVAC Tech Day (US):** Take a moment for the unsung heroes who ensure our air is conditioned, come summer or winter.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
-   **June 22, 1893 (IL):** The first Ferris wheel, standing a staggering 264 feet tall, officially opens at the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago. Talk about a revolutionary view!
-   **June 22, 1853 (WI):** Milwaukee's first streetcar line, pulled by horses, began operation, giving residents a whole new (and slightly slower) way to navigate the Cream City.

**US & World Historical Events**
-   **June 22, 1941 (World):** Operation Barbarossa begins, as Nazi Germany invades the Soviet Union, kicking off one of the largest and deadliest military campaigns in history.
-   **June 22, 1969 (US):** The polluted Cuyahoga River in Ohio famously catches fire (for the *eleventh* time!), igniting a spark in the burgeoning American environmental movement that would lead to the Clean Water Act.

### 23 JUN
**On this day — Tuesday, June 23**
Today we honor athletic prowess, engineering minds, and pivotal shifts in American equality, all while staying adequately hydrated.

**Celebrations & Holidays**
-   **National Hydration Day (US):** Remember, water is your friend. Especially if you’re reading this in a heatwave.
-   **National Pecan Sandy Day (US):** Dive into a classic shortbread cookie, dusted with a nutty goodness.
-   **International Women in Engineering Day:** Celebrating the ingenious women building our future, one girder and algorithm at a time.
-   **Olympic Day (International):** A global celebration of sport and the Olympic values of excellence, friendship, and respect.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
-   **June 23, 1888 (IL):** The first telephone exchange in Springfield, Illinois, opens, bringing local residents into the modern age of inconveniently long-distance conversations.
-   **June 23, 1860 (WI):** A devastating tornado sweeps through Baraboo, Wisconsin, leveling homes and businesses, reminding early settlers of nature's formidable power.

**US & World Historical Events**
-   **June 23, 1894 (World):** The International Olympic Committee (IOC) is founded in Paris by Pierre de Coubertin, paving the way for the modern Olympic Games we know and love.
-   **June 23, 1972 (US):** President Nixon signs Title IX of the Education Amendments into law, dramatically prohibiting sex-based discrimination in any school or education program receiving federal funding—forever changing the landscape for women in sports and academia.

### 24 JUN
**On this day — Wednesday, June 24**
From mysterious aerial phenomena to groundbreaking infrastructure connecting two key states, today is a day for expanding horizons.

**Celebrations & Holidays**
-   **National Pralines Day (US):** Indulge in these sweet, nutty confections, best enjoyed with a generous cup of coffee.
-   **National Upcycling Day (US):** Give new life to old things, because even a faded denim jacket deserves a second chance at fashion.
-   **St. John's Day / Midsummer Day (Traditional):** A historic day of celebration, often linked to ancient summer solstice festivities and bonfires.
-   **Swim a Lap Day (US):** Dive in! A refreshing reminder to get some exercise, or at least pretend you did.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
-   **June 24, 1904 (IL/WI):** The first interurban railway connecting Chicago and Milwaukee began service, shrinking the distance between these two powerhouse cities and making weekend trips a tangible reality.
-   **June 24, 1836 (WI):** The first capitol building of the Wisconsin Territory was established in Belmont, a crucial early step on the path to statehood.

**US & World Historical Events**
-   **June 24, 1947 (US):** Kenneth Arnold, a private pilot, reports seeing nine disk-like objects flying in formation near Mount Rainier, Washington, coining the term "flying saucer" and kicking off the modern UFO craze.
-   **June 24, 1948 (World):** The Soviet Union initiates the Berlin Blockade, cutting off all land and water routes to West Berlin, a tense flashpoint that defined the early Cold War.

### 25 JUN
**On this day — Thursday, June 25**
Today marks the start of major conflicts, the establishment of iconic symbols, and one of America's most famous last stands.

**Celebrations & Holidays**
-   **National Strawberry Parfait Day (US):** Layers of fruit, cream, and crunchy goodness make this a sweet summer treat.
-   **National Catfish Day (US):** Originally proclaimed by President Reagan in 1987, celebrating this distinctly American fish and its burgeoning aquaculture industry.
-   **Global Beatles Day:** Twist and shout! A day to celebrate the enduring legacy and music of the Fab Four.
-   **National Leon Day (US):** "Noel" spelled backward, this marks the halfway point to Christmas. Start your holiday shopping... or don't.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
-   **June 25, 1896 (IL):** The Supreme Court of Illinois rules that women are eligible to be admitted to the bar, a significant legal victory that expanded professional opportunities for women in the state.
-   **June 25, 1913 (WI):** The first hydroplane flight in Wisconsin takes place on Lake Monona in Madison, showcasing early aviation's marvels to an astonished crowd.

**US & World Historical Events**
-   **June 25, 1876 (US):** The Battle of Little Bighorn, famously known as "Custer's Last Stand," takes place in Montana, resulting in a decisive victory for Lakota, Northern Cheyenne, and Arapaho warriors over the U.S. Army.
-   **June 25, 1950 (World):** The Korean War officially begins as North Korean forces invade South Korea, drawing the United States and other UN forces into a brutal conflict.
-   **June 25, 1978 (US):** The original rainbow flag, designed by Gilbert Baker, is first flown at the San Francisco Gay Freedom Day Parade, becoming a vibrant and enduring symbol of LGBTQ+ pride.

### 26 JUN
**On this day — Friday, June 26**
From the birth of global cooperation to the quiet revolution of a grocery store checkout, this Friday delivers a diverse historical punch.

**Celebrations & Holidays**
-   **National Chocolate Pudding Day (US):** A creamy, comforting classic. Just try not to get it on your face.
-   **National Beautician's Day (US):** A day to appreciate the talented professionals who make us look and feel our best.
-   **United Nations Charter Day (Global):** Commemorates the signing of the UN Charter, a cornerstone of international peace and cooperation.
-   **National Camera Day (US):** Grab your lens! A day to capture memories and appreciate the art and technology of photography.

**Wisconsin & Illinois History**
-   **June 26, 1964 (IL):** Lincoln Land Community College is established in Springfield, Illinois, marking a significant step in providing accessible higher education to the region.
-   **June 26, 1856 (WI):** Milwaukee's first gas street lighting is introduced, bringing an end to the city's long reign of darkness and ushering in brighter, safer evenings.

**US & World Historical Events**
-   **June 26, 1945 (World):** The United Nations Charter is signed in San Francisco by 50 nations, laying the groundwork for a new era of international diplomacy and collective security.
-   **June 26, 1974 (US):** The first Universal Product Code (UPC) barcode is scanned on a 10-pack of Wrigley's Juicy Fruit gum at a Marsh supermarket in Troy, Ohio, forever changing the way we shop and track inventory.
-   **June 26, 2015 (US):** In a landmark decision, the U.S. Supreme Court rules in *Obergefell v. Hodges* that the right to marry is guaranteed to same-sex couples by the U.S. Constitution, legalizing same-sex marriage nationwide.
